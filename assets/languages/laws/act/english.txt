<h1>Primary Act:</h1>

<h2>Domestic Animals Act 2000</h2>
<h2>Sections Applicable:</h2>
<h2>Section 104, Rights of Persons Accompanied by Assistance Animals</h2>
<h2>Section 105, Exclusion of Assistance Animal from Public Place</h2>
<h2>Section 106, Imposition of Excess Charges for Assistance Animal</h2>
<h2>Excerpt from Section 104:</h2>

<p>“    (1) Despite any other territory law (other than the Discrimination Act 1991 ), a person with a disability accompanied by an assistance animal has the same right of access to, and the same right to the use of, a public place as a person who is not accompanied by an assistance animal.
        (2) Without limiting subsection (1), a person with a disability does not commit an offence merely by taking an assistance animal onto or into, or allowing the animal to enter, a public place. “
</p>

<h2>Maximum Fine for Breach of Act:</h2>
<h2>10 penalty units</h2>
<h2>Value of One Penalty Unit as of 2012:</h2>
<p>$110.00</p>
<h2>Exceptions to Access under Act:</h2>
<p>• It is not unlawful to impose a charge to a person with a dog guide if expense is reasonably occurred by the third person because of the presence of the dog guide.</p>
