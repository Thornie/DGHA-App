<h1>Primary Act:</h1>

<h2>Guide, Hearing and Assistance Dogs Act 2009</h2>
<h2>Section/s Appliccable:</h2>
<h2>Section 8, People with a Disability May be Accompanied by Their Guide, Hearing and Assistance Dogs</h2>
<h2>Excerpt from Section 8:</h2>
<p>“(1) Despite any other Act, a person with a disability who relies on a guide, hearing or assistance dog to reduce the person’s need for support may be accompanied by the guide, hearing or assistance dog in a public place or public passenger vehicle.
    (2) Also, a person with a disability who relies on a guide, hearing or assistance dog to reduce the person’s need for support does not commit an offence merely by taking the guide, hearing or assistance dog into a public place or public passenger vehicle. “
</p>

<h2>Maximum Fine for Breach of Act:</h2>
<h2>2 Penalty Units</h2>
<h2>Value of One Penalty Unit as of 2012:</h2>
<p>$100.00</p>
<h2>Exceptions to Access Under Act:</h2>
<p>
<ul>
    <li>an in-patient ward</li>
    <li>a labour ward</li>
    <li>a procedure room</li>
    <li>a recovery area</li>
    <li>an area in which the standard of hygiene is maintained at a significantly high level for the
        purpose of preventing infection or the spread of disease</li>
    <li>an ambulance</li>
    <li>a part of a public place or public passenger vehicle where food is ordinarily prepared</li>
    <li>It is not unlawful for a person with an dog guide to be asked to produce evidence to certify that their dog is an assistance animal.
            Other Legislation:</li>
    <li>Anti-Discrimination Act 1991</li>
    <li>Building Units and Group Titles Act 1980.</li></li>    
           
</ul>
</p>
