
<h1>Primary Act:</h1>
<h2>Guide Dogs and Hearing Dogs Act 1967</h2>
    <h2>Section/s Applicable:</h2> 
    <h2>3, Rights of Guide Dog Users and Trainers in Respect of Public Places and Transport</h2> 
    <h2>Excerpt from Section 3:</h2>

    <ul>
        <li>“(1) A person who is blind or vision impaired, deaf or hearing impaired or a trainer –</li>
        <li>(a) is entitled to be accompanied by a guide dog into any public place or onto any public passenger vehicle; and</li>
        <li>(b) does not commit an offence by taking a guide dog into or onto, or permitting a guide dog to remain in or on, a public place or public passenger vehicle.</li>
        <li>(2) A person who is in charge of a public place or public passenger vehicle, or the servant or agent of a person in charge of a public place or public passenger vehicle, must not –</li>
        <li>(a) refuse to allow a person accompanied by a guide dog into that place or onto that vehicle; or</li>
        <li>(b) direct a person accompanied by a guide dog to leave that place or vehicle; or</li>
        <li>(c) deny accommodation or service to a person accompanied by a guide dog. “</li>
    </ul>

<h2>Maximum Fine for Breach of Act:</h2>
<p>20 penalty units</p>
<h2>Value of One Penalty Unit as of 2012:</h2>
<p>$130.00</p>
<h2>Exceptions to Access Under Act:</h2>
<p>• It is not unlawful for a person with an assistance animal to be asked to produce evidence that their dog is an assistance animal.</p>
